Richard Kaufman is a software engineer

Currently, I’m doing freelance full-stack web development.

Previously, I built tools that analyzed content sharing at Pictoline, a visual news & information media company.

In 2014 and 2015 I was the creative technologist at Flock, a digital advertising company. At Flock I also was the director of the Maker Lab, a rapid prototyping laboratory that worked closely with creative directors to turn ideas into prototypes ready to communicate ideas.

I build websites using Javascript, Node.js, React.js, Koa, and NoSQL databases.

I create interactive applications and data visualizations using Processing.

I invent objects for the Internet of Things using Arduino and electronics.

I'm obsessed with building communities. I co-organize Mexico's Javascript community with my friends, Latin America's largest programming meetup. Back in the day, I co-founded Students for Free Culture @ UPRM. I gather with my friends to hack technology and make art at the Laboratorio del Error Diseñado (Lab for Erroneus Design.)

If you'd like to talk to me, feel free to send me an email or to reach out on Twitter.
